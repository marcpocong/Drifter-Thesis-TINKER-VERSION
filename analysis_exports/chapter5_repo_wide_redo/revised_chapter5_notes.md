# Chapter 5 Redo Notes

## Why should Chapter 5 include useful figures outside the publication package?

The publication package is a thesis-facing subset, not the full evidentiary surface of the repository. A repo-wide review found strong non-publication assets that improve Chapter 5 in three ways:

- they show the prototype-development history instead of making the chapter appear to begin only at the final validation package,
- they strengthen the transport-to-product narrative by showing trajectory and diagnostic boards that were never promoted into the publication subset,
- they make Phase 4 support/context and DWH transfer-validation sections more visually complete without changing the evidence hierarchy.

Using those assets is appropriate as long as the chapter labels them honestly as prototype, comparator-only, support/context, archive-only, or governance material where applicable.

## How should those figures be described so they do not become overstated claims?

Use language such as:

- prototype-development figure
- exploratory transport-realism test
- provenance/support result
- legacy diagnostic figure
- historical workflow-development evidence

Avoid language such as:

- final spill-footprint validation
- public truth comparison for oil extent
- main validation case

Keep these interpretation limits explicit:

- drifters support transport realism and recipe selection only,
- drifters are not direct oil-footprint truth,
- prototype/legacy figures do not replace Mindoro B1,
- PyGNOME is never truth and remains comparator-only where used.
